Project Overview

This project involved developing a comprehensive go-to-market (GTM) strategy for a Principal Investigator's biomedical research project. The task required translating technical research into a compelling B2B business case, identifying target customer segments, designing a market entry strategy, and creating a viable commercialization roadmap.

Objective

Move a promising biomedical research project from laboratory to market. Develop a strategic framework for identifying customers, positioning the technology, and executing a successful market launch.

Go-To-Market Roadmap

Phase 1: Validation (Months 1-3)
Conduct customer discovery interviews with 20+ potential customers. Validate product-market fit. Identify early adopters and advocates.
Phase 2: Pilot Program (Months 4-6)
Execute 3-5 pilot deployments with lead customers. Gather case study data. Refine go-to-market messaging based on real-world feedback.
Phase 3: Soft Launch (Months 7-9)
Launch in primary market segment. Build sales team. Establish partnership channels. Generate initial revenue.
Phase 4: Scale (Months 10+)
Expand to secondary segments. Build brand awareness. Pursue strategic partnerships and M&A opportunities.

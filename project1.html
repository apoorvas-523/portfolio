Phase 1: Clinical Discovery

Problem Identification: Conducted clinical observations at Boston Medical Center neurosurgery department, identifying 12 distinct problems.

User Needs Translation: Systematically translated clinical observations into structured user needs, then prioritized to final solution.

Project Selection Criteria:

  • Team's combined interests & expertise
  • Diverse skillset alignment
  • MEng timeline feasibility
  • Resource & facility availability

Phase 3: Verification & Validation

Testing & Risk De-Risking:

  • PFMEA (Process Failure Mode & Effects Analysis)
  • Killer experiments for critical specifications
  • Multiple prototype iterations (v1, v2, v3)
  • Durability & reliability validation
  • Clinical user interaction testing

Phase 2: Ideation & Design Inputs

Requirements Traceability Matrix (RTM): Systematic mapping of user needs → design specifications.

Design Control Deliverables:

  • Design Inputs: User needs and clinical requirements
  • Design Outputs: CAD models, technical drawings
  • Specifications: Performance, safety, usability requirements
  • Design Controls: FDA-aligned V-cycle methodology

Phase 4: Commercialization Strategy

Full Market Pathway Development: Parallel to technical design.

  • IP Strategy: Patent landscape & protection planning
  • Regulatory: 510(k) submission pathway & timeline
  • Go-To-Market: Target customer identification & market entry
  • Business Model: Canvas & revenue stream analysis

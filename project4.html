Project Overview

This project demonstrates practical proficiency in CAD design and modern prototyping methodologies. I designed and fabricated a functional prototype using Onshape CAD software and 3D printing technology. The project showcases the complete design-to-manufacturing workflow, including design for manufacturability (DFM) principles and iterative refinement based on functional testing.

Objective

Create a functional prototype for a medical device component while optimizing for 3D printing manufacturability. The design needed to balance precision requirements with manufacturing feasibility and cost.

Design Process

1. Requirements & Specifications

Initial design requirements defined the scope and constraints:

  • Dimensional tolerances: ±0.5mm
  • Material compatibility: Biocompatible polymer
  • Assembly compatibility with other components
  • Cost target: <$15 per unit at scale
  • Manufacturability via FDM 3D printing

2. CAD Design & Modeling

Developed detailed 3D CAD model in Onshape with parametric design features, enabling rapid iteration and design modifications. The model included all critical dimensions, assembly interfaces, and manufacturing notes.

3. Design for Manufacturability

Applied DFM principles specific to 3D printing technology:

  • Minimized support material requirements
  • Optimized wall thickness for print quality
  • Included draft angles for layer adhesion
  • Strategic orientation planning for printing

Prototyping & Iteration

Multiple prototypes were fabricated and tested to refine the design:

Prototype v1: Initial Design

First working prototype identified manufacturing challenges with complex geometry. Support material consumption was higher than optimal.

Prototype v2: Design Refinement

Redesigned geometry to reduce support material. Added draft angles and optimized wall thickness. Improved fit with assembly partners.

Prototype v3: Final Optimization

Final iteration validated all tolerances and assembly interfaces. Demonstrated functional performance in application environment. Ready for manufacturing scale-up.
